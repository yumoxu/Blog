Probability

Probability can be used for 2 types of problems

  • Generation/prediction: Reasoning from causes to effects
  • Inference. Reasoning from effects to causes.

    • The phrase “at random”

    The phrase “at random” is ambiguous: technically, it just means there is some randomness involved, not necessarily that all outcomes are equally likely.

    Geometric distribution

    Definition: $P(X=x) = (1-p)^{n-1} p$

    It is often referred as a “family” of distributions because the formula contains a parameter $p$.

    Geometric distribution characterize: we repeat some random experiment until we succeed, and we are only interested in how long it will take to succeed.

    Bionomial distribution \& multinomial distribution

    Bionomial distribution

    If we have a Bernoulli variable $X$ and we repeatedly sample values from $P(X)$ (e.g., ﬂip a coin many times), we will get some number of 1’s (heads) and some number of 0’s (tails). The binomial distribution with parameters $p$ and $n$ describes the probability of getting a certain number of 1’s out of a total of $n$ samples when the probability of a 1 on a single sample is $p$.

    Multinomial distribution

    Extension: the “coin” has more than two outcomes.

    Poisson distribution

    Thing happens $λ$ times within a certain ﬁxed period of time, but the intervals between the occurrences are random.
